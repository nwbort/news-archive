Everyone's Talking About...

  • Slide 1 of 5

    Meme warfare

    AI-generated videos and memes criticising Israel and the United States' war in Iran have gained momentum on social media.

    Lego-style videos have millions of views on social media and have been re-shared by Iranian regime accounts.

    The group behind the "Lego-ganda" claim it is "totally independent" of Iran's government, but one representative says Tehran is a "customer".

    Unlike propaganda of the past, which took time to prepare, this is being generated quickly and at a greater scale.

    One expert says this is "information warfare", which has the power to shape domestic and international opinion "in a way that could be decisive".

  • Slide 1 of 3

    Government backdown

    A move that led to some older Australians paying $50 an hour for care like showering has been reversed.

    The federal government was warned some older people were forgoing other care to afford showers.

    Fully covering the extra care will cost $1 billion over the next four years.

  • Slide 1 of 3

    NRL training change

    Landmark safety guidelines for training will limit contact in the sport for the first time.

    Experts welcomed the change and neuroscientist Alan Pearce described it as a "good start".

    A junior rugby league parent says possible head knocks were in the "back of every parent's mind".

  • Slide 1 of 3

    Apple CEO

    Apple's chief executive Tim Cook is stepping down from the role.

    Mr Cook has held the role since 2011, succeeding the company's founder, Steve Jobs.

    Company veteran John Ternus will now transition to CEO.

  • Slide 1 of 3

    Australite

    An ancient australite has been found in the WA Goldfields.

    A prospector discovered the black object on the ground.

    The cosmic debris is about 800,000 years old.
