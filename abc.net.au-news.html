Everyone's Talking About...

  • Slide 1 of 3

    Bluesfest

    Byron Bay's famous festival has been called off with less than three weeks' notice.

    Organisers blame rising production, insurance and touring costs.

    A liquidator has been appointed, and ticketholders will be contacted about potential refunds.

  • Slide 1 of 3

    Oil supplies

    Australia's domestic oil production has plummeted by about 90 per cent since 2000. 

    At the same time, six major refineries have closed across the country.

    Aging infrastructure meant Australia could not compete with Singapore's high-tech refineries.

  • Slide 1 of 3

    Fertiliser prices

    Fertiliser prices are skyrocketing for Aussie farmers due to the conflict in the Middle East. 

    Grocery prices are not expected to spike for now.

    The price of Urea fertiliser has gone up by 20 per cent and may hit record highs. 

  • Slide 1 of 3

    Mojtaba Khamenei

    Ayatollah Ali Khamenei's son Mojtaba Khamenei has been appointed as Iran's new leader.

    The 56-year-old has massive influence in Iran and holds extremist and anti-Western views.

    The supreme leader has the final say on foreign policy and Iran's nuclear program.

  • Slide 1 of 3

    Travellers return

    The first commercial flight from the Middle East since the outbreak of the Iran conflict has arrived in Sydney.

    The Emirates flight from Dubai brought home 200 Australians, who had spent the past few days grappling with uncertainty.

    After clearing customs, there were emotional scenes as they were embraced by loved ones.

  • Slide 1 of 3

    Jackie O

    Jackie O has quit her breakfast radio show ending 25 years with co-host Kyle Sandilands.

    The radio host had been absent from the show after being berated by Kyle during a live broadcast. 

    The show has been taken off the air and Sandilands has been suspended for 14 days.
