Everyone's Talking About...

  • Slide 1 of 4

    Trump eyes Greenland

    US President Donald Trump and his team are discussing options for acquiring Greenland from Denmark.

    The White House says the use of the US military in pursuit of the goal is "always an option".

    European nations have pledged support for Greenland, which continues to reject annexation by the US.

    Danish Prime Minister Mette Frederiksen warned that an invasion would end the decades-old NATO alliance.

  • Slide 1 of 4

    Martyn recovers

    Former cricketer Damien Martyn has awoken from an induced coma after falling ill in Queensland.

    The 54-year old Test veteran was hospitalised with meningitis in December.

    Close friend Adam Gilchrist says Martyn is now recovering well after a 'miraculous' turnaround.

    Medical staff say they are hopeful he will soon be able to be moved out of intensive care.

  • Slide 1 of 4

    Macron cyberbullying

    A Paris court has found 10 people guilty of cyberbullying France's first lady, Brigitte Macron.

    The charges related to the spread of false info online, including allegations she was born a man.

    Delphine Jegousse, 51, played a key role in the rumours, and was given a six-month sentence.

    The Macrons have also filed a US lawsuit against right-wing influencer Candace Owens.

  • Slide 1 of 4

    Venezuela strikes

    US forces have seized Venezuela's now-deposed president Nicols Maduro and his wife from Caracas.

    Mr Maduro and wife Cilia Flores were taken to New York to face narco-terrorism charges.

    The attack followed months of escalating tensions between Venezuela and the US.

    Donald Trump says the US will 'run' Venezuela until there's a new government.

  • Slide 1 of 4

    Khawaja retirement

    Usman Khawaja will retire from international cricket after the Ashes.

    The Australian batter took aim at "racial stereotypes" that dogged him through his career.

    In a powerful press conference he also paid tribute to his family.

    He said his parents taught him "values before ambition" and to "be humble".
